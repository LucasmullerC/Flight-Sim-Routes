Understanding Demands and Subfleets

What are Demands?

  • Demands reflect the desired activity level for an airport.
  • They are divided into four types: Extreme Demand, Big Demand, Medium Demand, and Low Demand.

Selecting Demands for Airports:

  • Customize the activity of each airport by choosing the demand type in the Airports section.
  • If no demand type is chosen, all airports will be generated as Low Demand.

How Do Demands Work?

  • Demands influence the probability of an airport being included in the generated schedules.
  • Extreme Demand has a 100% chance, while Big, Medium, and Low Demands have decreasing probabilities.
  • Subfleets exclusively operate the chosen demands on the Subfleets page.

Practical Examples:

  • Type: B738, Operational Countries: US, Aircraft Hub: KJFK, Aircraft Demand: Extreme Demand

    • *This Aircraft will only operate to airports assigned with Extreme Demand in the US.

  • Type: A20N, Operational Countries: BR, AR, Aircraft Hub: SBGR, Aircraft Demand: Extreme, Big, Medium Demand

    • *This Aircraft will only operate to airports assigned with Extreme, Big and Medium Demand in Brazil and Argentina.
